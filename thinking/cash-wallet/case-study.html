01  ·  OVERVIEW

A verified investor who never deposits is a missed investment

PM Note

An estimated 80% of KYC-verified investors had never deposited. The deposit flow's email step had no regulatory basis: it was a manual workaround for broken reconciliation. Fixing activation meant fixing that infrastructure, not the screens.

[Company] is a vertically integrated alternative assets platform: deal sourcing, tokenisation, custody, exchange, and secondary market. The deposit flow required users to bank transfer to a static DBS account, email a screenshot to cs@[company].com, and wait up to 5 working days with no feedback. That flow broke at two points.

01

Post-KYC Dead Zone

After KYC approval, users received no contextual prompt, no deal recommendation, no yield signal. They re-entered a cold, undifferentiated product state with no trigger to act.

02

Deposit Execution Loop

Users were pushed off-platform, required to type static account details, then email a proof screenshot. The email step had no regulatory basis. It existed because automated reconciliation didn't.

02  ·  CONTEXT

MAS regulation shapes the solution space

[Company] is MAS-licensed for capital markets services. This defines what can and cannot be built.

Regulatory Constraint

Funds must be traceable to the investor's own verified bank account. Card funding, third-party processors, and digital wallets are out. PayNow and bank transfer are the only compliant rails. The solution had to make those work better, not replace them.

Why the proof email could be eliminated

The email existed to trigger reconciliation and create an audit trail. Neither required user action. Unique per-transaction references capture payer identity automatically. Automated reconciliation is a better audit trail than screenshots: system-generated, tamper-proof, immediately auditable.

Permissible in Phase 1

Automated PayNow reconciliation via unique reference codes. Unique bank transfer references per transaction. Real-time in-app deposit status. Withdrawal legibility panel. Automated rep notifications.

Requires review before build

MMF yield rate messaging in onboarding (MAS promotional framing guidelines). Open banking API deposit rail (dedicated MAS consultation required; Phase 2 at earliest).

Critical assumption to validate

DBS PayNow integration supports unique per-transaction reference codes at [Company]'s current account tier. If not, automated PayNow reconciliation is blocked and a fallback is needed.

Out of scope

Multi-currency FX conversion, full withdrawal flow redesign, open banking APIs. Each requires a dedicated workstream before design or engineering investment is justified.

03  ·  USERS

Three types of verified investor, three different failure modes

Three investor archetypes failed at different points. The passive wealth allocator stalled because he couldn't confirm what happened after he transferred. The curious first-timer lost momentum when KYC approval led to a cold, undifferentiated home screen. The sophisticated diversifier routed capital elsewhere when 5-day confirmation silence made deal deadlines unconfirmable. Designing for the most trust-sensitive user raises the floor for all three.

04  ·  SOLUTION

Fix the transaction enabler first. Build the financial home second.

Phase 1 is scoped to existing payment rails without regulatory changes or cross-product dependencies.

Contextual Post-KYC Activation

KYC approval triggers a personalised activation screen with a deal recommendation, MMF yield explainer, withdrawal legibility panel, and a single deposit CTA. For rep-referred users, the RM's contact details appear inline.

In-App Deposit Initiation

Unique PayNow QR per transaction. Unique reference code per bank transfer, formatted for copy-paste. Amount pre-filled from deal. Deadline shown as a progress indicator.

Automated Reconciliation

Inbound transfers matched automatically via reference code. Proof email eliminated. Exceptions trigger an ops alert. System-generated audit logs satisfy MAS AML/CFT requirements better than screenshots.

Real-Time Status Tracking

In-app status stepper: Initiated, Transfer Detected, Funds Confirmed, Wallet Credited. Push notification at each state change. Tracker visible on wallet home screen. Automated rep notification on confirmation.

Withdrawal Legibility

Expandable panel shown before the user commits to deposit: steps, timelines, conditions. For Raymond, knowing he can withdraw is a prerequisite to depositing.

MMF Surfacing

Current yield rate displayed on wallet home screen for any investor with idle balance. Explained inline in the activation flow. Most users had no idea this existed.

Phase 2

Deal-triggered funding nudges, a wallet health dashboard (idle balance, deployed balance, MMF yield accrued), and an open banking deposit rail (pending MAS review).

06  ·  METRICS

What success looks like, and why

The North Star is scoped to deposit, not investment completion. Investment outcomes depend on deal quality and market timing, outside the wallet PM's control.

07  ·  LEARNINGS

What this work surfaced

The proof email was a symptom of a reconciliation gap, not a compliance requirement. Removing it required building what was missing, not negotiating what existed.

Compliance was brought in as a co-author of the exception spec rather than a late reviewer, which converted a potential launch blocker into a two-week design sprint.

08  ·  SIGNALS

Signals and what they tell us

Success is KYC-to-deposit conversion reaching 40% within 30 days, paired with a 60% drop in CS deposit confirmation tickets. The latter isolates causation in a way the North Star can't. The lagging health check is AUC growth: conversion rising while AUC stays flat signals low-intent activations, not genuine wallet adoption.

WHAT IT CLARIFIED

The work established that the proof email was an operational patch, not a product feature. Removing it required building automated reconciliation, not designing a better confirmation screen. Phase 1 was scoped entirely to what existing payment rails could support without regulatory change. Whether the activation problem is primarily an infrastructure failure or a post-KYC dead zone problem is the question Phase 1 data would answer.
