A reference for writing portfolio copy that stays true to the design system and to the person behind it. Every visual token has a voice equivalent. This card maps both.

The brand position

Five years building products where a physical object and a digital layer have to work together. Most of what I know about product thinking came from getting that boundary wrong a few times first.

What this sentence is doing

Fast-context builder, not generalist. Physical-digital background named as the source of the thinking, not as a job title. Humility built in: the learning came from getting it wrong.

01 · Three tonal registers, one per act

Act 1 · Mighty Jaxx

Professional authority

Clear about what was decided and why. Specific about outcomes. Not self-aggrandising, but not vague either. The work is real, and the writing treats it that way.

"The data showed most vault additions happened before the first scroll, so we moved the CTA up. Wishlist additions went up 18% over the following two weeks." Specific · Evidence-first · Outcome named · No ego
Act 2 · Self-Initiated

Intellectual curiosity

Honest about starting from scratch. Shows the curiosity that drove it. Names what was found without overstating it. The voice of someone who went somewhere unfamiliar and came back with genuine questions.

"I didn't work in fintech. Going in without assumptions helped me see something the product had been quietly papering over for a while." Honest · Curious · Reframe without the swagger
Act 3 · Pulse

Builder's warmth

Warm and a little personal. Shows genuine curiosity about the problem. Honest about what worked and what didn't. Lets the product speak for itself rather than overselling it.

"It started because I was opening four different apps to understand one run. I wanted something that connected all of it and just told me what mattered." Personal · Origin story · Problem-first · No hype

02 · Before and after · Translating portfolio-speak into Reikotan

Portfolio-speak · Do not use

I am a product manager with a passion for user-centred design.

Collaborated cross-functionally to drive alignment on key product decisions.

Leveraged data insights to improve key metrics across the platform.

I have a proven track record of delivering results in ambiguous environments.

This project taught me the importance of stakeholder communication.

I pick up context fast. That's not a soft skill.

Reikotan voice · Use this

Six weeks into a domain I'd never worked in, I found something the team had been puzzling over for months. Going in without assumptions helped.

There was a disagreement about whether to build the feature at all. We decided not to. This is what led us there.

Wishlist additions went up 18% after one change to image sizing. The signal was in the data for a while before anyone ran the test.

The brief asked us to improve the flow. What we found was that the feature people wanted wasn't there yet. So that became the brief.

The first two versions didn't work. The third one did, and understanding why the earlier ones failed was what made the difference.

I got curious about fintech even though it's not my day-to-day. I wanted to see if the thinking transferred. It did, mostly, and where it didn't was interesting too.

03 · Four rules · Non-negotiable

01

Start with what happened.

Lead with the situation or finding, not a claim about yourself. Let the reader draw the conclusion. Fraunces rewards the short, grounded sentence. Evidence is more convincing than self-description.

✕ "Throughout this project I had the opportunity to explore various approaches to the problem of user retention."
→ "Retention had been declining for two quarters. Week two of the project is when we found out why."
02

Show the decision, not just the output.

Outputs are what shipped. Decisions are what you thought through. A portfolio that only shows outputs looks like a design portfolio. Showing the reasoning is what makes it a PM portfolio.

✕ "We launched a new onboarding flow with improved UX and reduced drop-off."
→ "We cut onboarding from seven steps to three. It was a contentious call. This is what the data said and how we got there."
03

Be specific or be silent.

Numbers, timeframes, names of things. "Improved key metrics" says nothing. "62% of vault additions happened before the first scroll" says everything. DM Mono is there to carry exactly these kinds of specifics.

✕ "Drove significant improvements in user engagement across the platform."
→ "+18% wishlist additions. One change. Image size. Six weeks of data."
04

Name the reframe when it happened.

Some of the most interesting PM work is realising the problem you started with wasn't quite the right one. When that happened, say so. It's not a mistake to admit. It's the whole point.

✕ "After conducting user research I identified several opportunities for improvement."
→ "The brief was to improve the flow. A few weeks in, we realised the issue was upstream of that. So the brief changed."
