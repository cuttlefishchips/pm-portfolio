Getting verified investors to their first deposit

01 - Overview

A verified investor who never deposits is a missed investment

Why this problem, why now: Wallet activation was the binding constraint on AUM growth. At the time of this work, an estimated 80% of KYC-verified investors had never deposited. Every one of them was stranded capacity on a platform where revenue scales with deployed capital.

[Company] is a vertically integrated alternative assets platform: deal sourcing, tokenisation, custody, exchange, and a secondary market. Accredited investors can access private equity, private credit, funds, luxury assets, and real estate from SGD 500.

The problem: a meaningful share of KYC-verified investors never made a first deposit. The current flow required users to manually bank transfer to a static DBS account, email a screenshot to cs@[company].com, and wait up to 5 working days for confirmation with no feedback. That flow broke at two points.

01

Post-KYC Dead Zone

After KYC approval, users received no contextual prompt, no deal recommendation, no explanation of the idle yield benefit. They re-entered a cold, undifferentiated product state with no trigger to act.

02

Deposit Execution Loop

At the moment of intent, users were pushed off-platform to internet banking, required to type static account details, then email a proof screenshot. The email step had no regulatory basis. It existed because automated reconciliation didn't.

The work here covers the full product thinking: problem framing, personas, journey analysis, solution design, requirements, and UX.

02 - Context and Constraints

MAS regulation shapes the solution space

[Company] is MAS-licensed for capital markets services and operates as a recognised market operator in Singapore. This defines what can and cannot be built.

The key regulatory constraint: Funds must be traceable to the investor's own verified bank account. This eliminates card funding, third-party processors, and digital wallets as deposit rails. PayNow and bank transfer are the only compliant options, which means the solution had to make those rails work better, not replace them.

Why the proof email could be eliminated

The email step existed to trigger reconciliation and create an audit trail. Neither purpose required user action. Unique per-transaction payment references capture payer identity automatically. The audit trail is better satisfied by automated reconciliation than by screenshots: system-generated, tamper-proof, immediately auditable.

Permissible in Phase 1

Automated PayNow reconciliation via unique reference codes. Unique bank transfer references per transaction. Real-time in-app deposit status. Withdrawal legibility panel. Automated rep notifications.

Requires review before build

MMF yield rate messaging in onboarding (MAS promotional framing guidelines). Open banking API deposit rail (dedicated MAS consultation required; Phase 2 at earliest).

Critical assumption to validate

DBS PayNow integration supports unique per-transaction reference codes at [Company]'s current account tier. If not, automated PayNow reconciliation is blocked and a fallback is needed.

Out of scope

Multi-currency FX conversion, full withdrawal flow redesign, open banking APIs. Each requires a dedicated workstream before design or engineering investment is justified.

03 - Users

Three types of verified investor, three different failure modes

The product serves accredited investors across a wide range of sophistication and intent. The activation problem looks different for each of them.

R

Raymond

Passive Wealth Allocator

56, semi-retired, referred by his Phillip Capital RM. Expects a human somewhere in the loop. The deposit email arrives with a deadline and no guidance on what happens next. He calls his RM.

P

Priya

Curious First-Timer

34, fintech PM, newly accredited. Digital-first and exploratory. Signed up, completed KYC, then got busy. With no deal recommendation or yield signal post-approval, the momentum evaporated and she never came back.

M

Marcus

Sophisticated Diversifier

47, family office. Has a specific deal in mind and a fill window closing. Treats deposit as infrastructure. The 5-day confirmation silence means he can't confirm his position is secured, so he routes capital elsewhere.

Research I would run to validate this

CS ticket analysis

Pull deposit-related support tickets and tag by type. Expecting "I sent the transfer but haven't heard back" to dominate, which would confirm the confirmation black hole as a widespread problem rather than an edge case.

Session recording review

Review post-KYC sessions for scroll depth and time-to-action. A sharp drop within the first 90 seconds would indicate a motivation problem, not a navigation one, and point to the dead zone as the primary culprit.

User interviews: 5 recently-verified investors

One interview per persona archetype, focused on the post-KYC moment and the deposit attempt. Representative questions: What did you do immediately after getting approved? What stopped you from depositing? What would you need to see before putting money in?

Designing for Raymond raises the floor for everyone. If the product earns his trust through clarity, it more than satisfies Priya and Marcus along the way.

04 - Solution

Fix the transaction enabler first. Build the financial home second.

Phase 1 is deliberately scoped to what can be built within existing payment rails without regulatory changes or cross-product dependencies. It addresses every identified failure zone and is measurable against the North Star within 30 days of launch.

Contextual Post-KYC Activation

KYC approval triggers a personalised activation screen, not a generic home state. Deal recommendation based on stated preferences, MMF yield explainer, withdrawal legibility panel, and a single deposit CTA. For rep-referred users, the RM's contact details appear inline.

In-App Deposit Initiation

Unique PayNow QR per transaction for SGD. Unique reference code per transaction for bank transfers, formatted for copy-paste. Amount pre-filled from deal. Deadline shown as a progress indicator, not a pressure notification.

Automated Reconciliation

Inbound transfers matched automatically via reference code. Manual proof email eliminated. Exceptions trigger an ops alert, not user action. System-generated audit logs satisfy MAS AML/CFT requirements better than screenshots.

Real-Time Status Tracking

In-app status stepper: Initiated, Transfer Detected, Funds Confirmed, Wallet Credited. Push notification at each state change. Deposit tracker visible on wallet home screen without navigating to transaction history. Automated rep notification on confirmation.

Withdrawal Legibility

Expandable panel within the deposit onboarding flow, shown before the user commits. Steps, timelines, conditions. For Raymond, knowing he can get his money out is a prerequisite to putting it in.

MMF Surfacing

Current yield rate displayed on wallet home screen for any investor with idle balance. Explained inline in the activation flow. Idle capital earns while the investor decides. Most users had no idea this existed.

Phase 2 direction: Once activated depositors are funded, the wallet needs to become a place they choose to keep money. Deal-triggered funding nudges, a wallet health dashboard (idle balance, deployed balance, MMF yield accrued), and an open banking deposit rail (pending MAS review) are the Phase 2 priorities.

06 - Metrics

What success looks like, and why

The North Star is scoped to deposit, not investment completion. Investment outcomes depend on deal quality, market timing, and member firm relationships. Outside the wallet PM's control.

07 - Learnings

What this work surfaced

01

The proof email was hiding a reconciliation problem, not solving one

The email step looked like a UX problem. It was an infrastructure gap: no automated reconciliation. The fix was eliminating the need for it, not redesigning around it.

02

Withdrawal legibility is an activation lever, not a withdrawal feature

No investor said they abandoned because they couldn't withdraw. But for the Passive Allocator, not knowing is a silent blocker. It never shows up in drop-off data because the user never deposits in the first place.

03

The MMF is a differentiator that most users have never heard of

Idle balances earn yield automatically. Competitors don't offer this at scale, and most users had never heard of it. It wasn't in the onboarding flow or on the wallet screen. Surfacing it is a communication fix, not a product change.

04

Regulatory constraints shaped the solution, but they didn't block it

MAS source-of-funds requirements eliminate most alternative payment rails. Working within them, making PayNow and bank transfer work better, was sufficient for Phase 1. The constraint clarified the design.

05

The North Star needs a lagging health check

Aggressive prompting can inflate deposit count with low-intent users. Pairing the North Star with AUC growth as a lagging indicator catches this. Conversion rising while AUC stays flat means the quality of activation is the problem, not the volume.

06

Compliance was the hardest stakeholder, and the most important to get right early

Compliance wanted to keep the proof email as an audit record. The reframe: automated reconciliation is a better audit trail than screenshots. System-generated, timestamped, immediately queryable. Bringing them into the design sprint as co-authors of the exception spec, not reviewers at the end, changed the dynamic. The two-week delay upfront avoided a much longer one at launch.

08 - Signals & Iteration

Signals and what they tell us

This is a proposed solution. Here is what the data would need to show to confirm the approach is working, and what a course correction looks like if it doesn't.

Green lights

North Star moving within the first sprint

KYC-to-deposit conversion climbing toward 40% within 30 days. The earlier leading indicator: PayNow's share of total deposits rising in week one. That's the friction removal showing up before the full window closes.

CS ticket volume dropping

Deposit confirmation tickets falling 60%+ in the first two weeks confirms the black hole was the problem, not something adjacent like withdrawal confusion. It also isolates causation in a way the North Star can't.

Re-engagement emails becoming less necessary

Fewer users reaching the 48-hour drop-off window means re-engagement open rates should fall. A declining open rate is normally a warning sign. Here it means the product is doing the re-engagement job.

Warning signs and the response

Conversion up, AUC flat

Low-intent users: depositing the minimum, not returning. The fix is adding intent signals to the activation flow: investment horizon, deal preference, minimum commitment. Also worth checking whether MMF surfacing is drawing savings-account behaviour rather than investment intent.

Auto-reconciliation rate below 90%

An edge case in the reference code mechanism, most likely users editing the reference field on bank transfer. Immediate response: manual reconciliation queue with a tight ops SLA. Medium-term: matching algorithm using amount and payer identity as fallbacks. No need to pause the launch.

30-day login rate below 30%

Fund-and-disappear persists after activation. The post-deposit experience isn't creating a reason to return, which moves the Phase 2 wallet dashboard from deferred to urgent. Diagnosis first: is the problem informational (they don't know what's in the wallet), intentional (deposited for one deal, nothing else to act on), or trust-based (waiting for a first successful withdrawal before committing more)?
