01 - Overview

A verified investor who never deposits is a missed investment

Why this problem, why now: An estimated 80% of KYC-verified investors had never deposited. Wallet activation was the binding constraint on AUM growth.

[Company] is a vertically integrated alternative assets platform: deal sourcing, tokenisation, custody, exchange, and secondary market. The deposit flow required users to bank transfer to a static DBS account, email a screenshot to cs@[company].com, and wait up to 5 working days with no feedback. That flow broke at two points.

01

Post-KYC Dead Zone

After KYC approval, users received no contextual prompt, no deal recommendation, no yield signal. They re-entered a cold, undifferentiated product state with no trigger to act.

02

Deposit Execution Loop

Users were pushed off-platform, required to type static account details, then email a proof screenshot. The email step had no regulatory basis. It existed because automated reconciliation didn't.

02 - Context and Constraints

MAS regulation shapes the solution space

[Company] is MAS-licensed for capital markets services. This defines what can and cannot be built.

The key regulatory constraint: Funds must be traceable to the investor's own verified bank account. Card funding, third-party processors, and digital wallets are out. PayNow and bank transfer are the only compliant rails. The solution had to make those work better, not replace them.

Why the proof email could be eliminated

The email existed to trigger reconciliation and create an audit trail. Neither required user action. Unique per-transaction references capture payer identity automatically. Automated reconciliation is a better audit trail than screenshots: system-generated, tamper-proof, immediately auditable.

Permissible in Phase 1

Automated PayNow reconciliation via unique reference codes. Unique bank transfer references per transaction. Real-time in-app deposit status. Withdrawal legibility panel. Automated rep notifications.

Requires review before build

MMF yield rate messaging in onboarding (MAS promotional framing guidelines). Open banking API deposit rail (dedicated MAS consultation required; Phase 2 at earliest).

Critical assumption to validate

DBS PayNow integration supports unique per-transaction reference codes at [Company]'s current account tier. If not, automated PayNow reconciliation is blocked and a fallback is needed.

Out of scope

Multi-currency FX conversion, full withdrawal flow redesign, open banking APIs. Each requires a dedicated workstream before design or engineering investment is justified.

03 - Users

Three types of verified investor, three different failure modes

R

Raymond

Passive Wealth Allocator

56, semi-retired, referred by his Phillip Capital RM. The deposit email arrives with a deadline and no guidance on what happens next. He calls his RM.

P

Priya

Curious First-Timer

34, fintech PM, newly accredited. Completed KYC, then got busy. No deal recommendation or yield signal post-approval. The momentum evaporated.

M

Marcus

Sophisticated Diversifier

47, family office. Has a specific deal and a fill window closing. The 5-day confirmation silence means he can't confirm his position is secured, so he routes capital elsewhere.

Research I would run to validate this

CS ticket analysis

Tag deposit-related support tickets by type. Expecting "I sent the transfer but haven't heard back" to dominate, confirming the confirmation black hole as widespread.

Session recording review

Review post-KYC sessions for scroll depth and time-to-action. A drop within the first 90 seconds points to the dead zone as the primary culprit, not navigation.

User interviews: 5 recently-verified investors

One per persona archetype, focused on the post-KYC moment and deposit attempt. Key questions: What did you do immediately after approval? What stopped you from depositing?

Designing for Raymond raises the floor for everyone. Earning his trust through clarity more than satisfies Priya and Marcus.

04 - Solution

Fix the transaction enabler first. Build the financial home second.

Phase 1 is scoped to existing payment rails without regulatory changes or cross-product dependencies.

Contextual Post-KYC Activation

KYC approval triggers a personalised activation screen with a deal recommendation, MMF yield explainer, withdrawal legibility panel, and a single deposit CTA. For rep-referred users, the RM's contact details appear inline.

In-App Deposit Initiation

Unique PayNow QR per transaction. Unique reference code per bank transfer, formatted for copy-paste. Amount pre-filled from deal. Deadline shown as a progress indicator.

Automated Reconciliation

Inbound transfers matched automatically via reference code. Proof email eliminated. Exceptions trigger an ops alert. System-generated audit logs satisfy MAS AML/CFT requirements better than screenshots.

Real-Time Status Tracking

In-app status stepper: Initiated, Transfer Detected, Funds Confirmed, Wallet Credited. Push notification at each state change. Tracker visible on wallet home screen. Automated rep notification on confirmation.

Withdrawal Legibility

Expandable panel shown before the user commits to deposit: steps, timelines, conditions. For Raymond, knowing he can withdraw is a prerequisite to depositing.

MMF Surfacing

Current yield rate displayed on wallet home screen for any investor with idle balance. Explained inline in the activation flow. Most users had no idea this existed.

Phase 2 direction: Deal-triggered funding nudges, a wallet health dashboard (idle balance, deployed balance, MMF yield accrued), and an open banking deposit rail (pending MAS review).

06 - Metrics

What success looks like, and why

The North Star is scoped to deposit, not investment completion. Investment outcomes depend on deal quality and market timing, outside the wallet PM's control.

07 - Learnings

What this work surfaced

01

The proof email was hiding a reconciliation problem, not solving one

It looked like a UX problem. It was an infrastructure gap: no automated reconciliation. The fix was eliminating the need for it, not redesigning around it.

02

Withdrawal legibility is an activation lever, not a withdrawal feature

No investor cited withdrawal as a blocker. But for the Passive Allocator, not knowing the exit path is a silent blocker that never appears in drop-off data because the user never deposits in the first place.

03

The MMF is a differentiator that most users have never heard of

Idle balances earn yield automatically. Most users didn't know. It wasn't in onboarding or on the wallet screen. Surfacing it is a communication fix, not a product change.

04

Regulatory constraints shaped the solution, but they didn't block it

MAS source-of-funds rules eliminate most alternative rails. Making PayNow and bank transfer work better was sufficient for Phase 1.

05

The North Star needs a lagging health check

Aggressive prompting can inflate deposit count with low-intent users. Pairing the North Star with AUC growth catches this: conversion rising while AUC stays flat means activation quality is the problem.

06

Compliance was the hardest stakeholder, and the most important to get right early

Compliance wanted to keep the proof email as an audit record. The reframe: automated reconciliation is a better audit trail. Bringing them in as co-authors of the exception spec rather than reviewers at the end turned a potential launch blocker into a two-week design sprint.

08 - Signals & Iteration

Signals and what they tell us

Green lights

North Star moving within the first sprint

KYC-to-deposit conversion climbing toward 40% within 30 days. Leading indicator: PayNow's share of total deposits rising in week one.

CS ticket volume dropping

Deposit confirmation tickets falling 60%+ in the first two weeks confirms the black hole was the problem. It isolates causation in a way the North Star can't.

Re-engagement emails becoming less necessary

Fewer users hitting the 48-hour drop-off window means re-engagement open rates should fall. A declining open rate is normally a warning sign. Here it means the product is doing the job.

Warning signs and the response

Conversion up, AUC flat

Low-intent users depositing the minimum and not returning. Add intent signals to the activation flow: investment horizon, deal preference, minimum commitment. Check whether MMF surfacing is drawing savings-account behaviour rather than investment intent.

Auto-reconciliation rate below 90%

Most likely users editing the reference field on bank transfer. Immediate response: manual reconciliation queue with a tight ops SLA. Medium-term: matching algorithm using amount and payer identity as fallbacks.

30-day login rate below 30%

Fund-and-disappear persists after activation, moving the Phase 2 wallet dashboard from deferred to urgent. Diagnose first: is the problem informational, intentional (one deal, nothing else to act on), or trust-based (waiting for a first successful withdrawal)?
