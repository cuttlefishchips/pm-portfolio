02 · Problem Diagnosis
Why do verified investors fail to deposit?
Three hypotheses — two internal, one external. Each points to a different intervention.
Internal · UX
No trigger at the highest-intent moment
KYC approval is peak intent. The current flow delivers no contextual prompt, no deal recommendation, no next step. Momentum evaporates before the user sees a deposit CTA. Friction here is the absence of a flow, not a flaw in one.
Internal · Process
Deposit execution breaks at the moment of action
Manual transfer to a static account + email screenshot as proof. Off-platform, signals operational immaturity, confirmation black hole up to 5 working days. For a first deposit, silence reads as failure.
External · Trust
Commitment anxiety on an unfamiliar platform
Accredited investors are deliberate, not unsophisticated. Depositing requires trust in a withdrawal process they haven't tested. Without upfront withdrawal legibility, inaction is rational — not friction.

Zone 01
Post-KYC Dead Zone
After approval, users enter a generic product state — no deal recommendation, no yield signal, no deposit prompt. The highest-intent moment is wasted. Users who don't act within 48 hours rarely return.
Zone 02
Deposit Execution Loop
At the moment of intent, users are pushed off-platform to internet banking, must type static details, then email a screenshot. The email step has no regulatory basis — it exists because automated reconciliation doesn't.

04 · Metrics
One North Star. Two operational signals.
North Star
% of KYC-verified investors who deposit within 30 days of approval
20% → 40%
Paired with AUC growth as a lagging health check. If conversion rises but AUC is flat, we are activating low-intent users — not genuine investors.
Operational Metric 1
90th percentile deposit confirmation time by rail
<2 hrs PayNow · <2 days bank
90th percentile, not median. Median masks the long-tail cases that erode trust most — the investor who waits 5 days and calls their RM is the one who never deposits again.
Operational Metric 2
30-day wallet login rate among first-time depositors
25% → 50%
Leading indicator of genuine engagement, faster to measure than repeat deposit rate. Detects "fund and disappear" behaviour early enough to act within the sprint.

05 · Feature Prioritisation
Three features. One unlocks the rest.
Ranked by impact on Time to First Deposit. Automated reconciliation is the foundation — without it, the others are cosmetic.
# Feature Impact Effort Rationale
1 Automated PayNow reconciliation + in-app deposit High High Eliminates proof email, kills the 5-day wait, keeps users on-platform at peak abandonment. Non-negotiable for Phase 1 despite the effort.
2 Contextual post-KYC activation flow High Medium Converts KYC approval momentum into deposit intent. Deal recommendation, yield signal, withdrawal legibility — primarily design and content. No new infrastructure.
3 Real-time deposit status tracking Medium Medium Closes the confirmation black hole. Push notification at each state change. Also eliminates the CS ticket volume from users asking if their transfer arrived.
05
06 · Top Feature MVP
Automated reconciliation: scope and hypothesis
Hypothesis
Replacing manual proof-of-payment with automated reconciliation via unique per-transaction reference codes will increase KYC-to-first-deposit conversion by 50% within 30 days of launch — measured against a holdback group on the existing flow.
In MVP
  • Unique reference code per transaction (PayNow + bank transfer)
  • Automated matching of inbound transfers to accounts
  • In-app deposit initiation with QR code and pre-filled amount
  • Four-state status tracker with push notifications
  • Ops alert for unmatched transfers
  • AML/CFT monitoring before wallet crediting
Out of MVP
  • Open banking API (requires MAS consultation)
  • Multi-currency FX conversion
  • Channel personalisation in activation flow
  • Wallet health dashboard
  • Deal-triggered funding nudges
  • Full withdrawal flow redesign

07 · Execution Plan
Three phases. Measurable at each gate.
Phase 1 · Pre-build
Validate and unblock
  • Confirm DBS PayNow unique reference code support
  • Compliance sign-off on automated reconciliation design
  • Legal review of MMF yield messaging
  • Baseline funnel data by channel and persona
Phase 2 · Controlled Rollout
10% of new KYC-verified investors
  • A/B against existing flow (holdback group)
  • Monitor reconciliation rate, confirmation time, CS tickets
  • Gate: 95%+ auto-reconciliation, no AML incidents
  • 5-10 user interviews on deposit experience
Phase 3 · Full Rollout · Success Criteria
100% of users
  • KYC-to-deposit conversion reaches 30%+ within 30 days
  • CS deposit query tickets down 50%+
  • 90th pct PayNow confirmation under 2 hours
  • No increase in AML flag rate vs baseline

08 · Cross-Functional Collaboration
Three teams. Three concrete asks.
Cross-functional plans fail when they stay at "I would collaborate closely with." Here is what I would actually do.
Growth and Marketing
Co-own the re-engagement sequence
Work with Growth to define re-engagement trigger logic — 7 days post-KYC, no deposit, deal matched to profile. Share the North Star as a joint goal. Agree on attribution definitions before launch, not after the metric ownership argument starts.
Compliance
Bring them in at design, not at review
Involve Compliance in the reconciliation design sprint as a contributor to exception handling — not a gate at the end. Pre-agree the MMF yield messaging framework before design begins. Avoids a late-stage redesign.
Operations and Customer Success
Treat ops readiness as a product requirement
The ops team owns the current manual proof email workflow — automating it removes their control point. Involve the ops lead in requirements so exception handling is designed to their workflow, not thrown over the wall. Success criterion: CS deposit tickets down 60% in 60 days.

09 · Trade-offs
Two tensions worth naming explicitly
Trade-offs are real when both sides have legitimate merit. These are the two I would want the team to consciously decide on.
Trade-off 01
Speed to market vs compliance certainty
Ship fast, patch compliance later vs Full sign-off before launch
Automated reconciliation touches AML/CFT — a domain where post-launch fixes are regulatory incidents, not bugs. My position: take the extra two weeks for pre-launch compliance sign-off. The downside of being wrong is not recoverable.
Trade-off 02
Activation volume vs activation quality
Maximise first deposit rate vs Only activate genuine investors
A strong re-engagement sequence can inflate the North Star with low-intent users who deposit once and disappear. This is why conversion is paired with AUC growth — if conversion rises but AUC is flat, we are manufacturing activations, not building a financial home.
