01 · The Problem

Granola stops at recall.
The real value is what happens after.

Granola has solved capture. Commitments stay buried in individual notes, organized by when the meeting happened, not by what still needs doing.

For users in 4–7 meetings daily, this means maintaining a parallel task system outside Granola, always slightly out of date. Granola’s co-founder has stated the ambition: “It is not about just capturing a meeting, but a space where you can work, even post meetings.”

02 · Key Decisions

The choices that shaped the feature

Three decisions where I chose a direction and rejected an alternative.

01
Precision over recall in extraction: conservative by default

If the first list contains noise, users conclude the feature is broken and don't return. Better to surface 6 high-confidence items than 12 that include noise.

Rejected Broad extraction with user-side filtering: puts the burden on users and confirms the feature is untrustworthy before trust is established.
02
60-day full trial, then paid only: no permanent free tier

A permanent free tier subsidizes non-converters and spends the pricing lever early. A capped preview creates friction at peak engagement. A full trial avoids both: the user accumulates 60 days of evidence before a conversion decision. Expiry is framed around items and history at risk, not feature marketing.

Rejected Permanent free tier with caps: adversarial conversion moment, eliminates future pricing flexibility, subsidizes non-converting users at scale.
03
The Anti-Jira Principle: Open Items must never require user input to function

Every addition must be additive, never required. If a user never touches a configuration option, Open Items must still be fully useful. Once users fill in fields to make the list work, it becomes a worse version of every task manager they already use.

Rejected Mandatory priority fields, required labelling, AI-inferred due dates: all violate the principle and erode what makes this distinctly Granola.
03 · The Solution

Open Items

A native Granola view that extracts personal commitments from meeting notes and surfaces them in one consolidated list, with source context. No setup. No configuration.

Vision: Transform Granola from a place where your meetings live into a system that ensures your meetings actually lead to something.

Each item carries the action text, a context snippet from the transcript, the source meeting and date, and a status: Open, Done, or Dismissed. Done and Dismissed are intentionally distinct: completing a task and deciding it's no longer relevant are different signals.

04 · Success Metrics

Measuring list trust,
not list completion

The north star is whether Open Items becomes part of the daily workflow. Completion rate within a fixed timeframe is explicitly not a metric.

North Star
Weekly Active Open Items Users / Weekly Active Granola Users

Whether the feature is part of the core workflow, not just tried once and abandoned.

Extraction Quality
Immediate Dismissal Rate

Items dismissed within 60 seconds. The strongest passive proxy for extraction noise.

Below 15%
Habit Formation
Session Entry via Open Items

% of daily active users opening Granola via Open Items first. Signals the feature has become load-bearing.

20% within 90 days
Monetization
Trial-to-Paid Conversion

% upgrading at or before trial expiry, benchmarked against Granola’s existing conversion rates.

Exceed existing benchmark
Explicitly Not Measuring

Completion rate within a defined timeframe. A 2-hour action and a 2-week follow-up can't be held to the same window. A fixed timeframe creates false precision and incentivizes extracting short-horizon items over genuinely useful ones.

05 · Tradeoffs

What we gave up and why

Two tradeoffs named explicitly. The full list is in the PRD.

Feature gravity locks the pricing decision

Once Open Items is in the paid plan, moving it to a higher tier later is nearly impossible. The window to treat it as a differentiator is at launch. After that it becomes table stakes.

Extraction quality is the only dependency that can kill the feature on day one

Noisy extraction at first impression doesn't recover at scale. The extraction prompt needs human-rated quality checks before launch, not just automated metrics.
